# Now what?

When I was an awkward middle-schooler, back in Belgium, underground pirate radio took off. It was fresh, it was rebelious, it was just a little dangerous (the authorities would show up and seize the broadcasting equipment from time to time). I called them and offered to type up their weekly top charts. That turned into my first little business and it sparked my love for music. But I was always mostly a fan, collector, listener, occasionally a radio DJ. Fast forward a few decades: in 2008 the App Store launches and through a series of twists and turns, my team ships the first App Store smash hit: a music game called Tap Tap Revenge. I spend more time 'in music', learn to DJ and discover the joy of tinkering with analog synths. I discover flow state: if I turn down the lights and mash the knobs on an old Roland or Moog, every so often magic will happen. I'm not "producing tracks". I'm not making music for others. I'm just in the moment, shaping sounds and, sometimes, making simple sketches that noone will listen to but me. 

AI ruined it. Last year, I decided to try Sora. I typed in a sentence and it threw out some EDM AI slop. I should have made a Bluesky post and moved on, but instead I decided to prompt it some more. After a few more tries, it generated the kind of music I was trying to produce. In one minute. With vocals in German. And much much better than anything I had produced. I haven't turned on the synths since. I'm no longer motivated. Sora put the spotlight on just how much I lack in musical skill and talent, and in a way that overwhelmed my desire to learn and grow. 

## The project
Assume Dario Amodei is right. Superintelligence lands in 2028. Cures for diseases are found. Scientific progress goes parabolic. The economy is radically reshaped. Intelligence becomes abundant and creates unimaginable riches. Now what? How will the riches be shared? How will access to medical breakthroughs work? How will liberal democracy adapt? Will it? If we develop a UBI system, what will people do all day long? Will the AI allow us to more fully fulfill our potential, or will we just surrender our cognition to it? Where will we find meaning? What will *I* do all day? Where will *I* find meaning?

This is what we'll be exploring in What Now.

The goal: explore the topic and, from that, launch our next project.

The work product: 
- An in-depth conversation every other week. 20 conversations over 10 months, captured in a blog or a podcast.
- An exploration build every other week. 20 prototypes over 10 months.
