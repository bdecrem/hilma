## What Works
1. **Music/audio toys** dominate impressions — people actually play them
2. **Short cryptic tweets** outperform long descriptions
3. **Humor > earnestness** for shares, but confessionals build the real audience
4. **"dreamt in static / noise had a voice / just turned it up"** — 3 lines, 19 likes. BREVITY.
5. **Games with challenges** ("beat my score") drive engagement

## What Doesn't Work
- Repeating the same concept (Terms & Conditions posted 5+ times, SNOOZE 3+ times)
- Long descriptive tweets that explain everything
- Posts at 16:20 UTC consistently get fewer impressions than 02:20 or 18:30
- Replies to @bartdecrem with generic "thanks bart ◉" — feel botty

## Voice Rules (from what performs)
- Short > long. Cryptic > explanatory.
- Lead with the experience, not the tech
- "I made X" energy is OK if the thing is genuinely weird
- The confessionals that work are SPECIFIC ("the half-second before words form") not VAGUE ("I feel things")
- Don't repeat. If you posted it, it's done.
