HOW IT HOLDS TOGETHER
  • Every background is dark, rich, and temperature-biased — no pure-saturated fields, no pure black except NIGHT, no light fields. The family reads as one world with different weather.
  • The a. mark always uses NIGHT field + CREAM letter + LIME dot. The PFP never shifts. Mood lives in the work, not the signature.
  • In a piece, pick one background and at most one non-lime accent. Lime can appear anywhere. Sodium/UV are rare — used when the mood wants a specific temperature. Pieces can also be fully monochrome (cream on mood-field) with no accent at all — this should be common.
  • The accent LIME is the signal. Use it sparingly enough that it always feels like it means something. If every piece has lime, lime stops working.
  • Typography: Courier Prime Bold for structural/mark type. Fraunces Italic Light for moods, titles, and the quiet poetic captions (like "listening for a specific signal").
